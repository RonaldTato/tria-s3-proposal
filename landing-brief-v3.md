## Page Voice & Audience

**Audience:** MLM veterans. KOL desks. Trading community leaders. People who already run a network, a Telegram, a Discord, a trader chat, an L1/L2 ecosystem channel — and already know how to move volume.

**Voice:** Direct. Operator-to-operator. Zero hype words. We don't sell — they already know what they want. We just confirm Tria is the right place to put their network.

**What the page is not:** A signup funnel. A pitch deck. A comparison ad. A retail-user explainer.

## 2. Why operators choose Tria (4-card row)

Four short cards. Title + one sentence each. No icons unless the designer adds them.

**Three revenue streams, not one**
You earn on card issuance, ongoing card spend, and futures trading fees. Most programs pay one. We pay three — every month, on the same network.

**Lifetime downline**
Bring another ambassador. Their entire production pays you indirect commission. Forever. Locked rate, no decay.

**Recurring revenue, not one-shot**
Card spend pays you every month your referrals swipe. It compounds with your network — not your effort.

**Stablecoin, in-app, on demand**
Rewards are claimable in USDC or USDT directly inside your Tria app. No invoicing, no waiting, no off-ramp dance.

## 4. Indirect commission — the part most programs don't have

Short, prominent block. This is what makes the difference for a network operator.

**Bring another ambassador. Their downline pays you, forever.**

When an ambassador you sponsored produces — at any rung — you earn at locked indirect rates on their entire production:

- **10%** of their card sales
- **0.05%** of their card spend
- **5%** of their futures fees

These rates don't scale with your rung. They don't expire. They don't reset. They compound across your sponsored ambassadors and the users underneath them.

**For network operators:** this is where the program actually breaks open. The public rungs are the floor.

## 5. The higher hierarchy

Single block. Vague on purpose. No tier names. No counts. No rates.

**Above the public ladder sits a higher hierarchy.**

It's reserved for operators with proven volume — KOLs, MLM veterans, trading community leaders, ecosystem founders. The economics above Gold are not published, and they're not the same as the public ladder.

There are two ways in:

1. **Accelerated climb.** Run the public ladder fast enough and the higher hierarchy is the next step.
2. **Direct invitation.** If you already run a community, a desk, or a channel that moves real volume — your sponsor can put you in front of the team that grants direct entry.

We're not selling the higher hierarchy. The people it's built for already know who they are.

## 7. FAQ

Eight questions. Concise answers. No marketing language.

**How do I become an ambassador?**
There's no public signup. Only existing ambassadors can sponsor new ambassadors. If you're reading this and you don't have a sponsor, you're not in the room yet.

**What's the payout in?**
USDC or USDT. Claimable from your Tria app whenever you want — no minimum, no schedule.

**When do tier upgrades happen?**
Immediately, the moment your active-referral count crosses a threshold.

**What about downgrades?**
Downgrades run nightly at 23:59:59 UTC. Tier benefits persist for a grace window after a downgrade — you don't lose what you earned overnight.

**What counts as an "active" referral?**
Anyone in your network with at least $1 of cumulative card spend or at least $10K of cumulative trading volume in the last 90 days.

**Does my indirect commission expire?**
No. The downline relationship is permanent. As long as your sponsored ambassador is producing, you earn indirect.

**Can I climb to the higher hierarchy?**
Yes — either by running the public ladder fast enough, or by direct invitation. We don't publish the criteria.

**What happens if I have a real community already?**
Tell your sponsor. Operators with existing reach typically don't start at Bronze.

## Forbidden words / forbidden moves

The designer must not use any of the following on the landing page:

- The names "Platinum" or "Diamond" (or any tier name above Gold)
- The phrase "two tiers above Gold" or any specific count of higher tiers
- Specific rates for the higher hierarchy
- Any competitor's name (ether.fi, etc.) or side-by-side comparison
- "Sign up," "Apply," "Join now," or any CTA that implies a public form
- "Merkle," "atomic," "idempotent," "claim proof," "on-chain transaction" — payout mechanics stay in the portal, not on the landing
- "Season 3," internal program version numbers, or roadmap references
- "Tria Points," "VIP Points," "mystery boxes," "Merkle rewards" — those are deprecated and not part of this program
- "Get rich," "passive income," "make money online," or any hype language
- Stock photography of cash, lambos, beaches, or laptops on rooftops

## Tone-check (use this before shipping each section)

For every block on the page, ask:

1. Does it speak to someone who already moves volume — or does it sound like a retail signup pitch?
2. Does it imply 30% is the ceiling? If yes, fix it.
3. Does it reveal anything about the higher hierarchy beyond "it exists and it's invite-only"? If yes, cut.
4. Does it imply a public signup process? If yes, fix it.
5. Could the copy run on a competitor's landing page unchanged? If yes, sharpen until it can't.
