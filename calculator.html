Why Tria beats ether.fi

Headlines below are sourced from the locked Tria specs only — every number is verifiable in tiers.md or ambassador/tiers.md. Nothing aspirational, nothing marketing-only. Use these as section heads / hero copy on the dedicated affiliate page.

Structural moat

Three commission streams, not one

Tria pays you on card sales, card spend, AND futures trading fees. ether.fi pays on cashback-card spend only — no futures, no card-purchase commission.

Sources: spec rate tables (both)
Real number

Up to 30% / 0.25% / 15% at Gold

Top user-side tier pays 30% on card sales, 0.25% of card transaction value, and 15% of futures fees. ether.fi tops out at 25% on physical card commission and 0.3% cashback on referee spend.

User Gold: 30% / 0.25% / 15% — locked
Structural moat

Indirect commission from day one

The moment you become an ambassador, you earn 10% card sales / 0.05% card spend / 5% futures on 2-hop activity in your tree. ether.fi gates indirect (Tier-2 revshare) behind 50 lifetime + 10 quarterly qualifying referrals.

Ambassador indirect: 10% / 0.05% / 5%
Structural moat

Both chain shapes count

Ambassador indirect pays on ambassador → ambassador → user AND ambassador → user → user. That closes the incentive gap most affiliate programs (including ether.fi's) leave open: you get paid for recruiting any active user two hops down, not just for recruiting other ambassadors.

Operational rigor

Active-user definition is precise

A user is active if cumulative card spend ≥ $1 OR cumulative trading volume ≥ $10K in the rolling 90-day window. ether.fi's "qualified referral" definition is vague ("Cash card activation + first $100 spend").

X_card $1 · X_trade $10K · N 90d
No silent expiry

Accrued balance held indefinitely

Earned commission never expires. If you're inactive, you still see your balance — only conversion to on-chain claim is gated. The instant you become active again, your full accumulated balance becomes claimable.

Structural moat

Exclusive ambassador tiers — Platinum & Diamond

Manually-granted tiers stack on top of the standard ladder, with no depth cap on ambassador-to-ambassador hops. A Diamond at the top of a 5-deep ambassador chain still earns on the leaf user's transaction. ether.fi has no analog.

Platinum: card sales 25–35% · card spend 0.3% · futures 20–30%
Anti-fraud built-in

Card-stream first-transaction gate

Card commission only starts accruing after the referee makes their first non-zero card transaction. Card issuance alone produces zero commission — closes the issuance-cost-without-revenue exploit loop.

Engineering discipline

Atomic, idempotent, reconcilable

Every claim traces back to underlying transactions. Webhook replays don't double-pay. Reversals are atomic. Merkle-claim payouts settle one of the smart-contract claim pipeline. ether.fi's published comms don't address any of this.

Persona-aware

Ambassadors bypass the claim-time gate

User-side claims require the claimant to themselves be active (it's a fair signal). Ambassadors — who are typically promoters, not heavy daily users — bypass that gate entirely. The system knows the persona difference and acts on it.

Structural moat

Five rungs vs ether.fi's four

Bronze-1 → Bronze-2 → Silver-1 → Silver-2 → Gold gives ambassadors finer-grained progression milestones — and a meaningful upgrade between 3 active referrals (the first earned tier) and 30 active referrals (Gold).

Thresholds: 0–2 / 3–9 / 10–19 / 20–29 / 30+
Smart cadence

Immediate upgrades, nightly downgrades

You climb the moment you cross a threshold. You don't drop until 23:59:59 UTC after a per-tier persistence window (configurable per tier) has elapsed. No "lost a sale and instantly demoted" panic moments.

The 30-second pitch

ether.fi pays you a small cashback on one product (cards). Tria pays you on three (cards sold, cards used, futures traded), at higher rates, with indirect commission unlocked from day one, on a five-rung ladder where you can't be silently demoted, with engineering rigor (idempotent accrual, atomic reversal, Merkle-claim payouts) ether.fi doesn't publish anywhere.
