What's "Average Pusher" vs "High Pusher"? +

Both bands assume your network is active — these are not "casual user" projections. The difference is how much each referral pushes on Tria each month. The math is identical; only the per-referral activity input differs.

Average PusherHigh Pusher
Monthly trading volume per ref$500K$2M
Monthly card spend per ref$2,000$5,000
Card-subscription sales per ref / year$200$1,000

Card-subscription sales = the gross dollar value of cards your network buys per year (new issuance, upgrades, replacements). Modeled at a middle-ground price between Virtual ($20), Signature ($90), and Premium ($225) — so the per-ref number absorbs the product mix. Calibrated against ambassador throughput: at a 30-ref Gold network, Average Pusher = ~5 cards/month; High Pusher = ~25 cards/month.

Average Pusher ≈ a crypto-native community where most referrals actually trade and use the card as a primary payment method. High Pusher ≈ a serious trader desk or KOL community — power users, Premium cardholders, real volume. Most active KOL networks land between these two, leaning toward High Pusher as the network matures.

What about sponsoring sub-ambassadors? +

Sponsored ambassadors pay you indirect commission on the customers they refer directly — at locked rates of 10% card-sale fees / 0.05% card spend / 5% futures fees — independent of your tier or theirs.

Important: standard tiers earn ONE level of indirect — your sponsored ambassador's direct customers. You don't earn from deeper layers. The "infinite downline" model is not how this program works.

Indirect commission compounds on top of the numbers above. Five sponsored ambassadors running networks similar to yours roughly double your annual run-rate; ten roughly triples it. Not modeled in the slider above — to keep the projection focused on your own direct activity.

How are rates applied? +

Card-sale commission — your tier % × the card price for every card subscription your network buys. Card prices and product mix are baked into the per-referral "card-subscription sales" assumption above.

Card-spend commission — your tier % × monthly card spend per referral.

Futures fee commission — your tier % × Tria's taker fee (0.05% of trading volume). Applied to fees, not to volume.

Tier thresholds at 3 / 10 / 20 / 30 active referrals, with Bronze 1 covering 0–2. Active referral = anyone in your network with ≥$1 card spend or ≥$10K trading volume in the last 90 days, depth 1 + depth 2.

Projections are estimates based on per-referral activity assumptions. Actual earnings vary with your network's behavior, the rate of card activations, and trading conditions. Calculator does not project the higher hierarchy above Gold.

Eight questions, no marketing. FAQ
01How do I become an ambassador?+
There's no public signup. Only existing ambassadors can sponsor new ambassadors. If you're reading this and you don't have a sponsor, you're not in the room yet.
02What's the payout in?+
USDC or USDT. Claimable from your Tria app whenever you want — no minimum, no schedule.
03When do tier upgrades happen?+
Immediately, the moment your active-referral count crosses a threshold.
04What about downgrades?+
Downgrades run nightly at 23:59:59 UTC. Tier benefits persist for a grace window after a downgrade — you don't lose what you earned overnight.
05What counts as an "active" referral?+
Anyone in your network with at least $1 of cumulative card spend or at least $10K of cumulative trading volume in the last 90 days.
06Does my indirect commission expire?+
No. The downline relationship is permanent. As long as your sponsored ambassador is producing, you earn indirect.
07Can I climb to the higher hierarchy?+
Yes — either by running the public ladder fast enough, or by direct invitation. We don't publish the criteria.
08What happens if I have a real community already?+
Tell your sponsor. Operators with existing reach typically don't start at Bronze.
